Explore Bandra

Mumbai's hip and trendy suburb with a perfect blend of history, luxury, and coastal charm.

About Bandra

Bandra is a coastal suburb located in the western part of Mumbai, known for its posh residential areas, trendy restaurants, and lively nightlife. It's often called the "Queen of the Suburbs" and is a perfect blend of old-world charm and modern amenities.

The famous Bandstand Promenade in Bandra with stunning sea views

History

Bandra's history dates back to the 16th century when it was a small fishing village. During the Portuguese rule, it became a significant settlement with many churches. After the British took over, it developed as a prominent suburb. Today, it's one of Mumbai's most desirable neighborhoods.

What to See

Bandra-Worli Sea Link

This iconic 5.6 km long bridge connects Bandra and Worli across the Arabian Sea. It's a marvel of modern engineering and offers spectacular views of Mumbai's skyline.

Bandstand Promenade

A popular seaside walkway that offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea. It's famous for celebrity homes, including Bollywood superstar Shah Rukh Khan's residence 'Mannat'.

Mount Mary Church

A historic Roman Catholic church that dates back to the 16th century. The annual Bandra Fair held here in September attracts thousands of devotees from all over.

Where to Eat

Pali Village Café

A rustic European-style café with exposed brick walls and vintage décor. Known for its delicious continental cuisine and relaxed ambiance.

Bastian

A popular seafood restaurant frequented by Bollywood celebrities. Known for its fresh seafood, creative dishes, and vibrant atmosphere.

Candies

A Bandra institution known for its eclectic décor and diverse menu. Perfect for breakfast or brunch, serving everything from sandwiches and salads to cakes and pastries.

Local Tips

  • Visit Bandstand during sunset for the most beautiful views of the Arabian Sea.
  • Explore the street art in Bandra, which is known for its vibrant murals and graffiti.
  • Shop at Linking Road or Hill Road for great fashion finds at affordable prices.
  • Try the East Indian cuisine, which is native to this area and has Portuguese influences.
  • Visit during the Bandra Fair in September to experience the local culture and festivities.
