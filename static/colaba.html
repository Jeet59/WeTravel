Explore Colaba

Mumbai's iconic southern neighborhood, home to historic landmarks and scenic waterfront views.

About Colaba

Colaba is the southernmost neighborhood of Mumbai, India. It is a vibrant district known for its colonial-era architecture, bustling markets, and iconic landmarks. The area is a popular tourist destination that offers a mix of historical sites, shopping experiences, and fine dining.

The Gateway of India, an iconic monument in Colaba

History

Colaba derives its name from Kolabhat, a word in the language of the Koli fishermen, the area's original inhabitants. During British colonial rule, it became an important military and commercial center. The southern tip of Colaba was once a separate island that was later connected to the main city by a causeway.

What to See

Gateway of India

Built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Mumbai, this iconic monument has become the symbolic entrance to India for visitors arriving by sea.

Taj Mahal Palace Hotel

This luxury hotel opened in 1903 and stands as a symbol of Indian hospitality. Its distinctive architecture combines Oriental, Florentine, and Moorish styles.

Colaba Causeway Market

A bustling street market offering everything from clothing and accessories to souvenirs and handicrafts. It's a paradise for shoppers looking for bargains.

Where to Eat

Leopold Cafe

A historic cafe and restaurant established in 1871. It's famous for its colonial charm, diverse menu, and its mention in the novel "Shantaram."

Bademiya

An iconic street food stall known for its mouthwatering kebabs and rolls. It's been serving delicious food since 1946.

Cafe Mondegar

Known for its jukebox, beer, and walls adorned with cartoons by Mario Miranda. It's a popular hangout spot for locals and tourists alike.

Local Tips

  • The best time to visit Colaba is early morning to avoid crowds, especially at the Gateway of India.
  • Bargaining is expected and part of the experience at Colaba Causeway Market.
  • Take a ferry from the Gateway of India to visit Elephanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • For a unique experience, try the Iranian cafes in the area, such as Kyani & Co.
  • Visit the Colaba Fish Market early in the morning to see local fishermen selling their fresh catch.
