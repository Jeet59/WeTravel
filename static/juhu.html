Explore Juhu

Mumbai's iconic beachfront suburb with celebrity homes, vibrant food scene, and gorgeous sunsets.

About Juhu

Juhu is one of Mumbai's most affluent and upmarket neighborhoods, famous for its expansive beach and as home to many Bollywood celebrities. This coastal suburb offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, entertainment options, and cultural experiences.

The popular Juhu Beach during sunset, a favorite among locals and tourists

History

Once a separate island, Juhu became connected to the rest of Mumbai during the colonial era. Its transition from a quiet fishing village to a glamorous suburb began in the mid-20th century when film personalities started building homes here. Today, it stands as one of the city's prime residential and recreational areas.

What to See

Juhu Beach

The heart of the neighborhood, this expansive beach offers stunning sunsets, horse rides, and a plethora of street food options. It's a perfect spot for evening walks and experiencing local culture.

ISKCON Temple

A spiritual haven dedicated to Lord Krishna, this architectural marvel attracts devotees and tourists alike. The temple complex includes a restaurant serving delicious vegetarian food.

Prithvi Theatre

A cultural landmark founded by Bollywood's Kapoor family, this intimate theater hosts plays, workshops, and has a lovely café that's a favorite among theater enthusiasts.

Where to Eat

Juhu Chowpatty Food Stalls

The beachfront is lined with food stalls serving Mumbai's famous street food like pav bhaji, bhel puri, and gola (ice treats). A must-visit for authentic local flavors.

Cafe Moshe's

A popular café known for its Mediterranean cuisine, freshly baked goods, and relaxed ambiance. Perfect for a quiet breakfast or lunch.

Mahesh Lunch Home

A renowned seafood restaurant serving Mangalorean cuisine. Known for its butter garlic crabs, prawns, and authentic coastal flavors.

Local Tips

  • Visit Juhu Beach during weekdays to avoid crowds, especially in the evening.
  • Carry cash for street food vendors who may not accept cards.
  • Try the famous "Mumbai sandwich" at the beach food stalls.
  • Check out Prithvi Theatre's schedule for cultural performances and events.
  • The best time to photograph Juhu Beach is during sunset when the colors are most vibrant.
