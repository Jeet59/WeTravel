Explore Dadar

Cultural heart with flower market and authentic street food

About Dadar

Dadar is one of Mumbai's fascinating neighborhoods with its own unique character and charm. Visitors can experience the local culture, explore markets, and enjoy authentic cuisine.

Exploring the vibrant streets of Dadar

What to See

Shivaji Park

A cultural and political landmark with historical significance, perfect for morning walks and witnessing local cricket matches.

Portuguese Church

A beautiful piece of architecture that adds contrast to the area, showcasing historical religious influences in Mumbai.

Dadar Flower Market

One of Mumbai's largest flower markets, bursting with colors and fragrances, best experienced in the early morning.

Where to Eat

Prakash Shakahari Upahar Kendra

A beloved spot for authentic Maharashtrian snacks and meals, particularly famous for its misal pav and thalipeeth.

Aaswad

An iconic restaurant serving traditional Maharashtrian cuisine, known for its award-winning misal pav and other local specialties.

Café Madras

A legendary South Indian restaurant serving authentic dosas, idlis, and filter coffee that attracts customers from across the city.

Local Tips

  • Walk between Dadar East and West via the overbridge—it's faster than a cab
  • Visit Dadar Market early morning for the freshest produce and flowers
  • Avoid Dadar station during rush hours (8-10 AM and 6-8 PM)
  • The area around Plaza Cinema has excellent street shopping
  • Try the street food near Siddhivinayak Temple, but be prepared for crowds
