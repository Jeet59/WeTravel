Colaba

South Mumbai's historic district with iconic landmarks

About

Colaba is a vibrant neighborhood in South Mumbai known for its iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India and the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel. This historic district features colonial-era architecture, bustling markets, and a vibrant street life.

Where to Eat

Leopold Cafe

Iconic cafe with a history dating back to 1871, serving continental, Chinese and Indian cuisines.

Bademiya

Famous street food joint known for delicious kebabs and rolls.

Cafe Mondegar

Popular cafe with Mario Miranda artwork, serving beer and international cuisine.

What to See

Gateway of India

Historic monument built during the British Raj, overlooking the Arabian Sea.

Taj Mahal Palace

Iconic 5-star luxury hotel with stunning architecture and history.

Colaba Causeway

Bustling market street with shops selling clothing, jewelry, and souvenirs.

Local Tips

  • Visit the Gateway of India early in the morning to avoid crowds
  • Bargain at the street shops on Colaba Causeway
  • Try the street food but be selective about where you eat
  • Take a ferry from the Gateway to visit Elephanta Caves
  • Visit the nearby Kala Ghoda art district for galleries and cafes

Bandra

The "Queen of Suburbs" with stylish cafes and vibrant culture

About

Bandra is an upscale coastal suburb known as the "Queen of Suburbs." This trendy neighborhood is home to Bollywood celebrities, heritage bungalows, stylish boutiques, and some of Mumbai's best restaurants and nightlife venues.

Where to Eat

Pali Village Cafe

Rustic European-style cafe with great ambiance and continental cuisine.

Candies

Popular cafe serving sandwiches, pastries and comfort food.

Lucky Restaurant

Old-school eatery known for Bandra's best Chinese and Indian cuisine.

What to See

Bandstand Promenade

Seaside walkway offering views of the Bandra-Worli Sea Link and Arabian Sea.

Mount Mary Church

Historic Roman Catholic church with beautiful architecture.

Bandra Fort

Ancient fort offering panoramic views of the coastline.

Local Tips

  • Visit Carter Road in the evening for street food and sea views
  • Explore the street art in Ranwar Village
  • Shop at the boutiques on Linking Road
  • Attend the Bandra Fair in September if possible
  • Try the East Indian cuisine at local restaurants

Juhu

Mumbai's famous beach destination with vibrant street food

About

Juhu is an upmarket neighborhood famous for its expansive beach along the Arabian Sea. It's popular among tourists and locals alike for its street food, celebrity homes, and recreational activities.

Where to Eat

Juhu Beach Food Stalls

Try Mumbai's famous pav bhaji, gola, and other street foods right on the beach.

Prithvi Cafe

Artistic cafe at the Prithvi Theatre, known for its Irish coffee and relaxed ambiance.

Mahesh Lunch Home

Renowned seafood restaurant serving authentic Mangalorean cuisine.

What to See

Juhu Beach

Long sandy beach with views of the Arabian Sea and vibrant street food scene.

ISKCON Temple

Spiritual center dedicated to Lord Krishna with beautiful architecture.

Prithvi Theatre

Cultural landmark hosting quality theatrical performances.

Local Tips

  • Visit Juhu Beach during sunset for the best experience
  • Try the pav bhaji and gola (ice dessert) from beach vendors
  • Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds
  • Check out Prithvi Theatre's schedule for cultural performances
  • Take a morning walk on the beach to see locals exercising

Andheri

Commercial hub with shopping centers and entertainment

About

Andheri is a bustling suburb and one of Mumbai's largest neighborhoods. It's divided into East and West, with Andheri East being a major commercial hub and Andheri West known for its shopping centers, restaurants, and nightlife.

Where to Eat

The Street Kitchen

Popular spot serving a variety of street foods from across India.

Yauatcha

Upscale Chinese restaurant known for dim sum and fine dining.

Bombay Brasserie

Contemporary Indian cuisine with regional flavors from across the country.

What to See

Versova Beach

Less crowded beach in the vicinity with a more local atmosphere.

Fun Republic

Entertainment complex with movie theaters, shopping, and eateries.

Lokhandwala Complex

Popular shopping area with numerous boutiques and shops.

Local Tips

  • Visit Lokhandwala market for shopping and street food
  • Check out Versova Beach for a quieter beach experience
  • Explore the food options in Andheri East's corporate parks
  • Use the metro to avoid traffic congestion
  • Visit Gilbert Hill, a 66-million-year-old monolith column of black basalt
